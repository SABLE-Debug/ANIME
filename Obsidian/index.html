05 — The Build Process
05   The Build

How To Build
An AI Employee

01
Define The Role
Identify the exact business function being replaced. One role per employee — no trying to make one AI do everything. Clarity of role equals quality of output. Sales Rep, Support Rep, Receptionist, Ops Assistant, or Marketing Assistant. Pick one. Build it perfectly.
02
Gather The Context
Interview the client for 60 minutes. Extract their tone of voice, common customer questions, escalation scenarios, what good looks like, what failure looks like. This becomes the AI Employee's knowledge base. The more context you gather, the better the output. This step is what separates AKHARA from generic chatbot builders.
03
Build The System Prompt
Write the AI Employee's brain using Claude API. Define persona, knowledge base, response logic, escalation triggers, and tone. This is the core IP — the thing that makes AKHARA's employees different. Spend 80% of your build time here. A bad system prompt produces a bad employee. A great one produces something that feels eerily human.
04
Connect The Channels
Use Make.com to connect to the client's existing tools — email inboxes, CRM systems, calendar apps, chat widgets, phone systems. No code required on the client side. They get a demo link and see their AI Employee working in their own environment on day one.
05
Test & Stress Test
Run 48 hours of internal testing. Try to break it. Submit edge cases, offensive inputs, out-of-scope questions. Document how it responds. Fix every failure before the client sees it. Nothing ships until it handles the worst case scenario gracefully. Your reputation depends on this step.
06
Deploy & Maintain
Go live. Target: 2 weeks from signed contract to live deployment. Then monthly or bi-weekly check-ins depending on tier. Review performance data, improve response quality, expand capabilities. This ongoing maintenance is what justifies the retainer indefinitely — and what clients can't do without you.

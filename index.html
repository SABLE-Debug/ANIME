The Build

How To
Build An
AI Employee

01
Define The Role
Identify the exact business function being replaced. One role per employee — no trying to make one AI do everything. Clarity of role = quality of output. Sales Rep, Support Rep, Receptionist, Ops Assistant, or Marketing Assistant.
02
Gather The Context
Interview the client for 60 minutes. Extract: their tone of voice, common customer questions, escalation scenarios, what good looks like, what failure looks like. This becomes the AI Employee's knowledge base. The more context, the better the output.
03
Build The System Prompt
Write the AI Employee's brain using Claude API. Define persona, knowledge base, response logic, escalation triggers, and tone. This is the core IP — the thing that makes AKHARA's employees different from a generic chatbot. Spend 80% of build time here.
04
Connect The Channels
Use Make.com to connect to the client's existing tools — email inboxes, CRM systems, calendar apps, chat widgets, phone systems. No code required on the client side. They get a demo link and see their AI Employee working in their own environment.
05
Test & Stress Test
Run 48 hours of internal testing. Try to break it. Submit edge cases, offensive inputs, out-of-scope questions. Document how it responds. Fix every failure before the client sees it. Nothing ships until it handles the worst case scenario gracefully.
06
Deploy & Maintain
Go live. Target: 2 weeks from signed contract to live deployment. Then monthly or bi-weekly check-ins depending on tier. Review performance data, improve response quality, expand capabilities. This ongoing maintenance is what justifies the retainer indefinitely.

How To Close The First Client
1
Build A Live Demo FirstBuild a working AI Employee for a fictional business in your target industry before you approach anyone. Let prospects interact with it directly. Seeing is believing. A live demo closes more deals than any pitch deck.
2
Find The Measurable PainAsk: "How many leads do you miss after hours?" or "What does your support backlog look like Monday morning?" Get them to put a number on the problem. Pain quantified becomes ROI justified. You're not selling AI — you're selling the solution to a number.
3
Present The Employee Not The ToolNever say "AI chatbot" or "software." Present the AI Employee by name and role. "This is your AI Sales Rep. It handles your leads between 6pm and 9am, qualifies them, and books meetings into your calendar." Frame it as a hire, not a subscription.
4
Collect The Setup Fee50% of first month upfront. Non-negotiable. If they push back on $500 for a $1,000/month service, they're not serious. This filter saves you from building for clients who will ghost on payment. Serious clients don't question it.
5
Deliver In 2 WeeksFrom signed contract to live AI Employee in 14 days maximum. Speed builds trust. Clients who see results fast become long-term retainers. Clients who wait 6 weeks become case studies in churn. Move fast, communicate daily, over-deliver on timeline.
